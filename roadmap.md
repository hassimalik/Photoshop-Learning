# 30-Day Photoshop Roadmap for Social Media & Personal Branding
**Daily commitment: 1 hour | Focus: Personal branding, social posts, headshots, cinematography**

---

## PHASE 1: Foundations (Days 1-7)

### Day 1: Interface & Workspace Mastery
**30 mins: Learn**
- Understand Photoshop workspace: Toolbox, Layers panel, Properties, Menu bar
- Navigate: File, Edit, Image, Layers, Select menus
- Zoom (Z key), Pan (Hand tool), Fit to screen (Cmd/Ctrl+0)
- Custom workspace for social media work

**30 mins: Project**
- **Task**: Open a photo. Resize it for Instagram (1080x1080px). Export as JPG.
- Practice: Cmd/Ctrl+Alt+I (Image Size), understand DPI (72 for social, 300 for print)

---

### Day 2: Lighting Fundamentals (Part 1) - Understanding Light & Shadow
**30 mins: Learn**
- Light direction: where does shadow fall? How does it sculpt the face?
- Hard vs soft light (shadows with hard edges vs gentle gradients)
- Highlight = brightest area (usually on subject)
- Shadow = darkest area (creates depth & dimension)
- Catch light = small bright spot in eyes (makes them "pop")
- Exposure vs contrast: exposure brightens all, contrast separates light/dark
- Histogram: visual representation of tonal range (darks left, lights right)

**30 mins: Project**
- **Task**: Open a portrait photo. Observe:
  - Where is the brightest light hitting?
  - Where are shadows? Do they sculpt the face well?
  - Are the eyes dull or do they have catch light?
  - Note: This is observation only. You'll manipulate next.
- Goal: Train your eye to see light

---

### Day 3: Lighting Fundamentals (Part 2) - Dodge, Burn & Shadow/Highlight
**30 mins: Learn**
- **Dodge tool** (D): Lighten specific areas (cheekbones, eyes, highlights)
- **Burn tool** (D): Darken specific areas (jawline definition, eyes, contours)
- Exposure & Range settings (shadows vs midtones vs highlights)
- **Shadow/Highlight adjustment** (Image > Adjustments > Shadows/Highlights)
  - Lift shadows without blowing highlights
  - Recover detail in blown-out areas
- **Curves adjustment** for precision: lift midtones, crush blacks, brighten specific tones
- Catch light creation: where to place highlights for eye impact

**30 mins: Project**
- **Task**: Retouch a portrait using Dodge/Burn:
  1. Use Burn on jawline/cheekbones (subtle definition)
  2. Use Dodge on forehead/nose to brighten
  3. Dodge the eye area to create catch light effect
  4. Apply Shadow/Highlight adjustment layer
  5. Darken background slightly with Burn
- Goal: Understand how lighting sculpts faces

---

### Day 4: Selection Tools & Isolation
**30 mins: Learn**
- Selection tools: Rectangle Select (M), Ellipse Select, Lasso (L), Magic Wand (W)
- Quick Select tool & Refine Edge
- Select → Inverse, Select → All, Select → Deselect
- Feathering & Anti-aliasing
- Select Subject (AI-powered, great for isolating people)

**30 mins: Project**
- **Task**: Find a photo with a person. Use Select Subject to isolate the person. Copy to new layer. Apply Dodge/Burn to subject only using layer mask.
- Goal: Precise lighting control on isolated subjects

---

### Day 5: Layers & Non-Destructive Workflow + Lighting Adjustments
**30 mins: Learn**
- Layer anatomy: Opacity, Blend modes (Normal, Multiply, Screen, Overlay)
- Layer groups (folder organization)
- Naming layers for clarity
- Lock layers (Lock position, Lock transparency)
- Rasterize vs Smart Objects
- **Using adjustment layers for lighting**: Curves, Levels, Exposure
- Blend modes for lighting effects: **Screen** (brighten), **Multiply** (darken), **Overlay** (contrast)

**30 mins: Project**
- **Task**: 
  1. Create 3 adjustment layers: Curves, Levels, Exposure
  2. Use Curves to lift shadows (brighten dark areas), crush blacks (deepen shadows)
  3. Use Levels to adjust highlight/shadow endpoints
  4. Add layer with Screen blend mode to brighten key areas
  5. Add layer with Multiply blend mode to deepen shadows
  6. Adjust opacity of each for control
- Goal: Non-destructive lighting enhancement

## PHASE 3: Advanced Techniques (Days 15-22)

### Day 15: Cinematography in Photography - Lighting as Visual Language
**30 mins: Learn**
- Cinematography principles applied to stills:
  - **Lighting direction**: Creates mood, drama, emotion (directional light = storytelling)
  - Frame composition (foreground, middle, background depth)
  - **Lighting & mood**: Low-key (dark, mysterious), High-key (bright, positive), Rembrandt (one side lit)
  - Color grading for mood (cool vs warm, desaturated vs vibrant)
  - Aspect ratios for different stories (16:9 cinematic, 4:3 classic, 1:1 intimate)
  - **Depth through lighting**: Bright subject on dark background creates separation
  - Leading lines & visual flow + light placement
  - Exposure balance for cinematic look: balanced exposure vs stylized

**30 mins: Project**
- **Task**: Take a portrait and grade it cinematically:
  1. Decide mood: dramatic, warm, cool, moody
  2. Use Dodge/Burn to adjust lighting (match mood)
  3. Add slight desaturation or color grade (Curves)
  4. Increase contrast for cinematic punch
  5. Add vignette for focus
  6. Export 3 versions: Dramatic vs Warm vs Professional
- Goal: Lighting as cinematic storytelling

### Day 17: Lighting for Thumbnails & Viral Social Posts

- **Contrast for impact**: Bright subject on dark background (or vice versa)
- **Exposure blending**: Combine multiple exposures for perfect balance
- Vignetting strategically (darker edges draw eye to center)
- Highlight placement: Where does the brightest spot go? (usually subject's face/eyes)
- **Clarity & texture**: Sharpness makes things "pop"
- Color contrast with lighting (warm highlights on cool background, etc.)

**30 mins: Project**

### Day 24: Advanced Headshot Retouching - Lighting & Professional Standards
**30 mins: Learn**
- **Frequency separation technique** (separate texture from color)
- **Dodge/Burn mastery for faces**: Sculpt cheekbones, jawline, nose bridge
- **Catch light perfection**: 
  - Professional catch lights are 2 small ovals (one in each eye)
  - Placed at 10-11 o'clock position (like overhead light)
  - Sized proportional to iris
  - Created with Dodge on separate layer
- **Exposure blending for perfect exposure**: Combine exposure for face + exposure for background
- **Rim lighting illusion**: Dodge hair edges to separate from background
- **Eye enhancement**: Darken iris (Burn), brighten white (Dodge), add shadow under upper lid
- Teeth whitening (Dodge + Desaturate) & subtle whitening
- Hair retouching (Burn for stray hairs, Dodge for highlights)
- Professional headshot standards: Even lighting, defined shadows, sharp eyes

**30 mins: Project**
- **Task**: Retouch a professional headshot using advanced lighting:
  1. Frequency separate for skin work
  2. Dodge/Burn face: Brighten cheekbones, define jawline, sculpt nose
  3. Create professional catch lights in both eyes
  4. Brighten under-eye area (makes face look awake)
  5. Add rim lighting along hair/shoulders
  6. Exposure blend face and background for perfect exposure
  7. Enhance eyes: Darken iris, brighten white, sharpen
  8. Export 2 versions: Social (1024x1024) + Print (300 DPI, 8x10")
- Goal: Hireable, professional headshot with perfect lighting

## Pro Tips for Efficient Learning

1. **Use Keyboard Shortcuts**: Learn 3 new shortcuts per week (Cmd/Ctrl+Z, T for type, M for selection, etc.)
2. **Study Good Design**: Follow designers on Instagram, analyze what they do in Photoshop
3. **One Technique Per Day**: Focus deeply rather than skimming many
4. **Save Everything**: Keep your PSDs organized by date/project
5. **Compare**: Always keep "before" versions to see your progress
6. **Consistency Over Perfection**: Done and published beats perfect and waiting
